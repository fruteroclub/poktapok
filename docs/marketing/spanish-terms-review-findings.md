# Spanish Terms Review — Findings and Change Proposals

**Document Reviewed:** frutero-marketing-design-master-document-q1-2026.md
**Review Date:** February 9, 2026
**Status:** Pending approval

## Executive Summary

This review identifies Spanish terms and concepts in the marketing master document that may not translate well, cause confusion, or require language consistency improvements. Issues range from critical (brand identity confusion) to minor (clarity improvements).

---

## Critical Issues

### 1. "We are Fruteros" (Line 38)

**Current Usage:**

> "Unity: 'We are Fruteros' creates shared identity."

**Problem:**

- Mixing English sentence structure with Spanish plural noun
- In English, this reads awkwardly and doesn't convey clear meaning
- "Fruteros" (plural) suggests multiple fruit vendors, which may not be the intended brand positioning

**Proposed Solutions (choose one):**

**Option A — Pure Spanish (Recommended):**

```
Unity: "Somos Frutas" / "Somos Frutero" creates shared identity.
```

- Most authentic for LATAM audience
- Clear cultural resonance
- Aligns with Spanglish strategy mentioned elsewhere in the document

**Option B — English Collective:**

```
Unity: "We are Frutero" creates shared identity.
```

- Treats "Frutero" as collective brand name (like "We are Google")
- Works better in English contexts

**Option C — English with Explanation:**

```
Unity: "We are Frutas" (we are the fruits) creates shared identity.
```

- Clearer metaphor in English
- Explains the fruit-based identity system

**Action Item:** Option A ("Somos Frutas" / "Somos Frutero") is most authentic for the target audience and aligns with the bilingual community strategy. Frutas for the individuals, Frutero for the community. Options B and C are good options for English-speaking context. Option A is quite universal as a strong Spanish statement "Somos Frutero" and "Somos Frutas" work in English and Spanish Marketing, "We are Frutero" / "We are Frutas" works for English with low Spanish-speakers exposure (Asia, Africa, East Europe).

## Brand Naming Consistency Issues

### 2. "Frutero" Brand Identity Clarity

**Current Usage:** The name "Frutero" appears in multiple contexts:

- Line 1: "Frutero — Marketing Design Master Document"
- Line 22: "Frutero's community moat"
- Line 52 (table): "Frutero (umbrella)" and "Frutero (quality)"
- Line 74: "Frutero.Club"

**Problem:**

- "Frutero" in Spanish literally means "fruit seller" or "fruit vendor" (person who sells fruits)
- For English-speaking audiences (investors, protocol partners), this might reduce perceived professionalism
- In B2B contexts, explaining "we're the fruit seller" could be confusing

**Context Notes:**

- The document embraces "anthropomorphic fruit mascots" (line 38)
- The "Certified Fresh, Organic Quality" tagline (line 52) leans into the fruit metaphor
- Brand voice is "raw and builder-first rather than corporate" (line 8)

**No Change Recommended — BUT:**
Consider preparing a brand narrative explainer for English B2B contexts:

```
"Frutero — In LATAM culture, the 'frutero' is the trusted local vendor who knows exactly
what's fresh, connects you to quality, and builds community. That's our role in the
AI talent ecosystem: we identify, cultivate, and connect LATAM's freshest tech talent
to global opportunities."
```

**Action Item:** Add a similar explanation to pitch decks and protocol proposals when presenting to low Spanish-speakers exposure audiences. Frutero (Fruit bowl/fruit storage) is a play on the diversity of fruits, and how each can provide a different dimension to a dish/drink. Likewise, our talent diversity provides different dimensions to any project, event, or organization, where any Fruta performs. Also, the Frutero (person that sells fruits in any "Mercado") knows which fruits are ripe and which are maturing, they know what you need.

### 3. "Sedes Frutero" vs "Sedes Fruteras" (Line 25)

**Current Usage:**

> "Sedes Frutero Network extends the Hub model"

**Problem:**

- Grammatical inconsistency: "Sedes" (feminine plural noun) paired with "Frutero" (masculine singular adjective)
- Should match gender and number

**Proposed Solution:**

```
Sedes Fruteras Network
```

OR

```
Red de Sedes Fruteras (Frutero Hub Network)
```

**Action Item:** Keep "Sedes Frutero" as a proper noun (brand name), we must treat "Frutero" as the brand identifier.

### 4. "Meetups Frutales" (Line 33)

**Current Usage:**

> "Meetups Frutales — ETHCDM provides structured quarterly Ethereum meetups"

**Problem:**

- "Frutales" means "fruit-related" or "fruit trees" in Spanish
- Could be interpreted as "Fruit Tree Meetups" rather than "Frutero Meetups"
- Inconsistent with "Frutero" brand naming in rest of document

**Proposed Solution:**

```
Frutero Meetups — ETHCDM
```

OR

```
Encuentros Fruteros (Frutero Meetups)
```

**Action Item:** "Meetups Frutales" is specific to a grant program by the Localism Fund + Ethereum Everywhere team. Otherwhise, they should just be called meetups.

## Minor Clarity Issues

### 5. "Casa Frutero" vs "Casa Frutera" (Line 24)

**Current Usage:**

> "Casa Frutero Hub (CDMX)"

**Grammatical Note:**

- "Casa" (house) is feminine, so grammatically it should be "Casa Frutera"
- However, "Casa Frutero" works if treating "Frutero" as a proper noun (brand name)

**Action Item** — Keep "Casa Frutero": acceptable as a branded location name (like "Casa Nike" or "Casa Tesla").
